<h2>Zenit</h2>
<p><b>A native, offline, private, AI-powered anti-procrastination tool for the Mac</b></p>

</div>

## How does it work?

When you would like to get some work done, you start a "Session" on Zenit. This is where you tell Zenit what you're working on.

<img width="450" height="414" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1823c9bf-2835-47b3-bdf3-4e566fcc7395" />

Once you start a session, Zenit will run in the background and will analyse the contents of your screen every minute to decide whether it is related to what you said you were going to work on.
It uses Apple Intelligence (running entirely on your Mac) to understand context, not just app names. E.g. opening Safari isn't procrastination, but watching YouTube when you should be revising is.

## What happens when you procrastinate?
Zenit gathers information about your screen content and other usage to give you a "Procrastination Score" from 0 to 100, where a higher score indicates that you are more likely to be procrastinating.<br>
Above a certain threshold, Zenit will nudge you in a tiered way:

**Level 1: You are slightly distracted**<br>
<img width="541" height="145" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/26aabd5a-eb95-4752-bfcd-9eecd1500b9b" /><br>
Zenit plays a sound and displays a notification in the top right of your screen, nudging you to get back on task. This notification displays on top of all of your windows and overrides Do Not Disturb in order to get your attention.

**Level 2: You are still distracted**<br>
Zenit will nudge you with the notification at an increasing frequency (around every 3 to 5 minutes)

**Level 3: You are very distracted**<br>
<img width="1917" height="1080" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f262db31-571b-43f7-81fb-e0d937b348f8" /><br>
Zenit displays a full screen overlay which displays over all of your apps on all of your displays. You have to type a randomly selected 5-letter word to dismiss it. The act of having to type out a random word to dismiss this popup aims to break your procrastination by making you aware in a not-so-subtle way that you have a task at hand. 

**Level 4: You are very distracted and have been ignoring Zenit for a while**<br>
<img width="1911" height="1080" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/00a8a916-97b0-4213-9571-307fefd21547" />
Zenit displays a full screen overlay which displays over all of your apps on all of your displays. This time, you have to answer a random trivia question generated by your on-device Apple Intelligence. You have to either answer the question correctly or wait 15 seconds for the "Skip and Dismiss" button to become available so you can dismiss it. This may sound like a gimmick, but it eliminates the problem of you getting used to typing out a five-letter word to dismiss the previous overlay, helping you to stop procrastinating.<br><br>
Zenit also begins to display overlays like this every one to two minutes, making it very hard to keep procrastinating.

**What if I stop procrastinating?**<br>
Zenit will not nudge you if it sees that your procrastination score is decreasing.

## Zenit is completely private
Everything Zenit does is completely private. It uses Apple Intelligence which comes with every Mac to carry out its analysis and does not send any analytic or usage data to any external servers. It also does not require an online account to use.<br><br>
<b>Please note:</b> Zenit contacts an API once the first time it is launched, and when you activate a licence for the first time in order to validate the free trial and the licence. This only happens once.

## Pricing
Zenit comes with a no-strings-attached 14-day free trial, where you can access all of the features and see if Zenit works for you. After the trial, Zenit requires a **one-time purchase** of £5.99 (or the equivalent in your local currency). All Zenit users get free updates forever.

## Requirements
Zenit requires a Mac with Apple Silicon (M1 or later) running macOS 26 or later.
It also requires that Apple Intelligence is enabled in System Settings.
